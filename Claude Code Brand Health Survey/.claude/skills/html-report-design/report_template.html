Executive Summary

  • 78.3% of respondents reported familiarity with the topic, indicating strong baseline awareness across the sample.
  • Opinion was cautiously negative, with only 31.2% expressing a favorable view — suggesting informed skepticism.
  • Age was the strongest demographic predictor of attitudes (chi-square test, p = 0.003), with younger respondents significantly more favorable.
  • A notable gap exists between awareness and adoption: while familiarity is high, only 12.4% reported active engagement.

Recommended actions: Focus engagement efforts on the 25-44 age demographic where both awareness and favorability are highest. Address the awareness-adoption gap through targeted information campaigns.

Key Findings

Theme 1: Awareness and Familiarity

"How familiar are you with [topic]?"

The survey revealed strong baseline awareness among respondents, with 78.3% reporting at least some familiarity. The distribution shows a concentration around moderate familiarity levels, suggesting broad but not deep engagement with the subject matter.

p = 0.003

Familiarity varied significantly by age group, with respondents aged 25-34 reporting the highest awareness (84.1%) compared to those aged 45+ (62.3%).

Theme 2: Attitudes and Opinion

Despite high awareness, overall sentiment was cautiously negative. Only 31.2% expressed a favorable opinion, while 42.8% were neutral and 26.0% unfavorable. This pattern of "informed skepticism" — high awareness paired with reserved opinion — was consistent across most demographic groups.

Conclusions and Recommendations

1. Awareness is high but engagement is low

The strong awareness levels (78.3%) have not translated into active engagement (12.4%). Recommendation: Develop targeted engagement programs that convert awareness into action, focusing on the 25-44 age group where favorability is highest.

2. Informed skepticism dominates the sentiment landscape

The combination of high awareness and reserved opinion suggests that respondents have formed evidence-based views rather than acting from ignorance. Recommendation: Address skepticism with transparent, evidence-based communications rather than awareness campaigns.

3. Age and education are the strongest predictors of attitudes

Both age (p = 0.003) and education level (p = 0.018) showed significant associations with key outcomes. Recommendation: Segment outreach strategies by age and education level to maximize effectiveness.
