# Employee Engagement Survey 2024 — Analysis Report

**Prepared:** January 15, 2025
**Prepared for:** HR Leadership Team

---

## Executive Summary

- **Overall engagement is strong but not universal:** **72.4%** of employees rated their overall engagement as 4 or 5 (out of 5), while **12.1%** rated it 1 or 2, indicating a meaningful disengaged minority.
- **Career development is the top concern:** Only **48.3%** of respondents agreed or strongly agreed that they have adequate career growth opportunities — the lowest-scoring item in the survey.
- **Manager quality drives engagement:** Satisfaction with direct manager was the strongest predictor of overall engagement, with a highly significant relationship (chi-square test, p < 0.001).
- **Tenure creates a satisfaction gap:** Employees with 5+ years of tenure reported significantly lower engagement (**64.2%**) compared to those with under 2 years (**81.7%**) (p = 0.008).
- **Remote workers report higher satisfaction:** Fully remote employees scored **8.2 points higher** on work-life balance than fully in-office employees (p = 0.012).

Based on these findings, we recommend prioritizing career development programs, particularly for mid-tenure employees, and investigating the drivers of the engagement decline after year 5.

## Methodology

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Original sample size | N = 1,247 |
| Cleaned sample size | N = 1,183 |
| Responses removed | 64 (51 incomplete, 13 duplicate) |
| Data collection method | Online survey via SurveyMonkey, distributed via company email |
| Analysis methods | Frequency analysis, cross-tabulation, chi-square test of independence (α = 0.05) |
| Analysis date | January 15, 2025 |

**Data quality notes:** 51 responses were removed due to more than 50% of questions left blank. 13 duplicate submissions (same email, submitted within 5 minutes) were removed, keeping the first submission. The final sample represents a 67% response rate of the 1,765 eligible employees.

**Key terms:**
- **Cross-tabulation**: A table showing how responses to one question break down across categories of another variable (e.g., satisfaction by department)
- **Chi-square test**: A statistical test that determines whether two categorical variables are related. A result is "statistically significant" (p < 0.05) when the observed pattern is unlikely to be due to chance alone.

## Key Findings

### Theme 1: Overall Engagement and Satisfaction

> "On a scale of 1-5, how would you rate your overall engagement at this company?"

The majority of employees expressed positive engagement, with **72.4%** selecting 4 or 5. However, **12.1%** rated their engagement at 1 or 2, representing approximately 143 employees who may be at risk of disengagement or turnover.

This varied significantly by tenure (chi-square test, p = 0.008). Employees with under 2 years of tenure reported the highest engagement (**81.7%** rating 4-5), while those with 5-10 years reported the lowest (**64.2%**).

**Table 1: Overall Engagement by Tenure**

| Rating | Under 2 yrs | 2-5 yrs | 5-10 yrs | Over 10 yrs | Overall |
|--------|------------:|--------:|---------:|------------:|--------:|
| 1-2 | 7.3% | 10.4% | 16.8% | 14.5% | 12.1% |
| 3 | 11.0% | 15.8% | 19.0% | 17.2% | 15.5% |
| 4-5 | 81.7% | 73.8% | 64.2% | 68.3% | 72.4% |

### Theme 2: Career Development Concerns

> "I have adequate opportunities for career growth and development."

Career development received the lowest scores across all survey items, with only **48.3%** agreeing or strongly agreeing. This represents a significant gap compared to other satisfaction measures, all of which exceeded 60% agreement.

The concern was particularly acute in Operations (**39.4%** agreement) compared to Engineering (**57.1%**) (p = 0.003).

### Theme 3: Manager Effectiveness

> "My direct manager supports my professional development."

Manager satisfaction was high overall (**76.8%** agreement), but showed significant variation by department (p < 0.001). Sales reported the highest manager satisfaction (**84.1%**) while Operations reported the lowest (**65.2%**).

## Conclusions and Recommendations

### Conclusion 1: Career Development is the Most Urgent Priority
With only **48.3%** agreement on career growth opportunities — 24 points below manager satisfaction — career development represents the biggest gap in the employee experience.
**Recommendation:** Launch a structured career pathing program, starting with Operations where only **39.4%** see adequate growth opportunities.

### Conclusion 2: Mid-Tenure Employees Need Attention
The significant engagement drop at the 5-year mark (from **73.8%** to **64.2%**, p = 0.008) suggests employees may hit a "ceiling" after initial growth.
**Recommendation:** Implement "stay interviews" for employees approaching their 5-year mark to proactively identify and address concerns.

### Conclusion 3: Remote Work Policies are Working
Remote employees report significantly better work-life balance (p = 0.012) without any significant difference in engagement or productivity measures.
**Recommendation:** Maintain and potentially expand flexible work arrangements.
