1. Executive Summary

Acme Strawberries achieved a significant brand health inflection between the 2025 and 2026 survey waves, with meaningful gains across every primary metric — awareness, purchase rate, brand preference, purchase intent, and advocacy. While absolute scores remain centered at the scale midpoint and NPS is still negative, the pace and breadth of improvement are commercially significant. The findings below represent the most critical takeaways from this tracking study.

  • Aided brand awareness surged 17.25 percentage points year-over-year, rising from 65.25% in 2025 to 82.5% in 2026 — the largest absolute awareness shift in the study. Unaided (spontaneous) recall also grew significantly, from 30.75% to 41.75% (+11 pp, p = 0.002), confirming that Acme is becoming more top-of-mind, not merely better recognized when prompted.
  • Purchase rate climbed 13.5 pp (28.5% to 42.0%) and brand preference doubled, rising from 15.95% to 34.38% — surpassing Crimson Crown Berries (formerly first at 30.81%, now 23.96%) to make Acme the top preferred brand for the first time. Concurrently, Crimson Crown's purchase rate fell 13 pp (48.75% to 35.75%), a pattern consistent with direct competitive switching.
  • Purchase intent shifted sharply from neutral to positive territory: the proportion of respondents indicating probable or definite future purchase nearly tripled, from 16.75% to 40.75% (p < 0.001). The Net Promoter Score distribution shifted dramatically toward higher scores, though overall NPS remains deeply negative at -35 (Promoters: 18.6%, Detractors: 53.8%), confirming Acme is mid-journey rather than a fully established brand.
  • Acme's perceptual gains are concentrated precisely where they matter most commercially. The three strongest drivers of purchase intent are overall quality (Pearson r = 0.44), freshness (r = 0.39), and sweetness/flavor (r = 0.29). Top-2-box agreement scores on quality more than doubled (25.75% to 52.5%) and freshness nearly doubled (29.25% to 50.25%) year-over-year — a strategically favorable alignment of improving perceptions with commercial importance.
  • The large neutral segment — 58.25% of all respondents chose the purchase intent midpoint — represents Acme's primary growth opportunity. Demographics (age, gender, and region) show virtually no significant variation in brand performance, confirming that this neutral audience is broad-based and reachable through general market investment rather than narrow segmentation.
Recommended Actions: Prioritize converting the neutral-intent majority through trial and quality-signaling programs that emphasize the sensory attributes (quality, freshness, and flavor) already shown to drive purchase intent, while building brand trust — which demonstrates disproportionate independent predictive power (standardized beta = 0.14) — through sourcing transparency and consistency messaging. Simultaneously, develop competitive messaging targeting Crimson Crown's remaining buyer base to capture the momentum of Acme's preference leadership.

2. Methodology

Study Design

The ACME Strawberries Brand Health Tracking Survey is a two-wave cross-sectional study designed to measure brand awareness, perceptions, purchase behavior, and competitive position for Acme Strawberries against six competitors in the fresh strawberry market. The six competitor brands tracked are: Crimson Crown Berries, Sunrise Berries, Red Valley Organics, FreshPick Strawberries, Golden Meadow Berries, and Harvest Hill Berries.

Sample

The two waves are equal in size by design, providing balanced year-over-year comparisons. All wave-level statistics use 400 as the denominator unless otherwise noted.

Data Collection

Respondents were screened for fresh strawberry category purchasers within the past six months. The survey instrument collected unaided and aided brand awareness, past-year purchase behavior across seven brands, brand attribute evaluations (11 attributes on a 5-point Likert scale), purchase intent, category and brand purchase frequency, most recent brand purchased, Net Promoter Score (0–10 scale), brand preference, and four demographic variables (age, gender, state, household income).

Analytical Methods

Frequency analysis: Response distributions (counts and percentages) were computed for all survey questions. Valid N is reported for each question; missing data is noted where applicable.

Cross-tabulation and chi-square testing: Cross-tabulations were produced for all question columns by five demographic variables: survey wave (year), age group (Q14), gender (Q15), state/region (Q16), and household income (Q17). A total of 191 chi-square tests of independence were conducted. Statistical significance was evaluated at the conventional alpha = 0.05 threshold. Chi-square tests assess whether the distribution of responses on a question differs significantly across demographic groups; a statistically significant result (p < 0.05) indicates the observed difference is unlikely to be due to chance alone.

Derived importance analysis (regression-based): Pearson correlation coefficients (r) and standardized regression beta coefficients were computed between each of the 11 Q5 brand attribute ratings and the Q6 purchase intent score, across all 800 respondents. Pearson r captures the overall (total) relationship between each attribute and purchase intent. Standardized beta coefficients, derived from a multiple regression model, capture each attribute's unique contribution to purchase intent after controlling for all other attributes. This method, known as derived importance analysis, identifies which attributes are most actionable for influencing purchase intent.

Data Quality Notes

  • Missing data on Q11 (brand preference): 46 of 800 respondents (5.75%) did not answer Q11, reducing the valid N to 754. Per-wave denominators for Q11 are 370 (2025) and 384 (2026).
  • Chi-square expected frequency warnings: Two significance tests have expected cell frequencies below the conventional threshold of 5 — the Q6 purchase intent × wave test (min expected frequency = 0.5, driven by only one respondent selecting score 1) and the Q10 NPS × wave test (min expected frequency = 3.5, due to the 11-point scale across two waves). Both results are overwhelming in magnitude (chi-square = 76.3 and 130.976, respectively) and the practical conclusions are unaffected, but precise p-values should be treated as approximate.
  • Additional assumption warnings: The income × quality cross-tab (p = 0.016, min expected frequency = 3.14) and the gender × shelf life cross-tab (p = 0.034, min expected frequency = 0.73) carry expected frequency warnings and should be interpreted with caution.
  • Demographic frequency tables: Standalone frequency distributions for age (Q14), gender (Q15), and state (Q16) were not produced by the data processor. Exact demographic composition of the sample cannot be reported for these variables. Income group totals were derived from banner table column sums.

3. Respondent Profile

The study includes 800 respondents screened as fresh strawberry purchasers within the past six months. The sample is split evenly across two survey waves (400 per wave). All respondents answered the screener criterion, confirming active category participation.

The sample skews toward middle- and higher-income households, with approximately three-quarters of respondents reporting household incomes of $50,000 or above. This income profile is broadly consistent with primary grocery shoppers.

Age (Q14)

Standalone frequency data for age is not available from the files produced. For cross-tabulation analysis, age was collapsed into three bands: 18–34 (combining 18–24 and 25–34 year-olds), 35–54 (combining 35–44 and 45–54 year-olds), and 55+ (combining 55–64 and 65 or older).

No statistically significant age-related differences were found on core brand metrics (awareness, purchase behavior, or brand attribute evaluations). Two borderline exceptions were observed: age versus Harvest Hill Berries aided awareness (p = 0.041) and age versus the sustainability attribute (p = 0.039); see Section 5 for details.

Gender (Q15)

Standalone frequency data for gender is not available from the files produced. For cross-tabulation, gender was collapsed to three groups: Male, Female, and Other (combining Non-binary, Prefer not to say, and Other). No statistically significant gender effects were found on awareness, purchase, or brand evaluation metrics, with one borderline exception — gender versus the shelf life attribute (p = 0.034, with an expected frequency warning indicating this result may be unreliable).

State / Region (Q16)

Standalone frequency data for state is not available from the files produced. For cross-tabulation, states were grouped into three regions: Northeast/Midwest, South, and West. Zero statistically significant regional differences were found across all 19 regional cross-tabulations tested (all p > 0.05), confirming that Acme's brand performance is geographically broad-based.

4. Key Findings

The findings below are organized by theme, reflecting the most analytically coherent groupings of the data rather than question-by-question order. Statistical significance is assessed using chi-square tests of independence; a result is considered statistically significant when p < 0.05, meaning there is less than a 5% probability the observed difference is due to chance.

Theme 1: Acme Strawberries Is on a Steep Upward Trajectory

Acme's aided awareness surged 17.25 percentage points year-over-year and its purchase rate jumped 13.5 points, with brand preference flipping from second to first place.

The most consequential finding of this study is the scale and breadth of Acme's year-over-year improvement. Brand awareness, purchase behavior, and brand preference all moved significantly in the same direction — and at the same time.

Aided brand awareness — recognition of Acme when shown a list of brands — grew from 65.25% (261 of 400 respondents) in 2025 to 82.5% (330 of 400) in 2026, a gain of 17.25 percentage points (chi-square test, p < 0.001, chi2 = 29.95). This is the largest absolute awareness shift observed in the study. At the same time, unaided brand recall — the ability to name Acme spontaneously, without prompting — grew from 30.75% (123/400) to 41.75% (167/400), an 11 percentage point gain (p = 0.002, chi2 = 10.00). The growth in unaided recall is particularly meaningful because it indicates Acme is becoming more top-of-mind, not merely better recognized when prompted.

Crucially, these awareness gains translated directly into commercial outcomes. Purchase rate rose from 28.5% (114/400) to 42.0% (168/400) — a 13.5 pp gain (p < 0.001, chi2 = 15.38). Brand preference, measured by asking respondents which brand they would choose if all were equally priced, shifted even more dramatically: Acme rose from 15.95% (59/370 valid respondents) to 34.38% (132/384), surpassing Crimson Crown Berries to become the top preferred brand for the first time (p < 0.001, chi2 = 34.37).

Q11 (brand preference) had 46 non-responses out of 800; per-wave denominators are 370 (2025) and 384 (2026).
The causal mechanisms driving awareness growth — such as earned media, distribution expansion, or advertising — cannot be determined from survey data alone. These metrics confirm that growth occurred; they do not explain why.

Theme 2: Perception Improvements Are Concentrated in Acme's Most Commercially Valuable Attributes

Acme's sharpest perceptual gains occurred on the attributes that matter most to purchase intent — quality, freshness, consistency, and trust — all improving significantly year-over-year.

Not all perceptual improvements are equally valuable. What makes Acme's 2026 gains particularly strategically significant is that the attributes that improved most sharply are precisely those with the strongest demonstrated links to purchase intent (see the Key Drivers of Purchase Intent section below).

Overall quality perception — the single strongest driver of purchase intent — more than doubled on top-2-box agreement (scores 4 or 5 on the 5-point Likert scale): from 25.75% in 2025 to 52.5% in 2026, a gain of 26.75 percentage points (p < 0.001, chi2 = 76.61). Strongly agree scores (score 5 only) rose from 4.25% to 17.5%.

Freshness perception — the second strongest purchase intent driver — nearly doubled on top-2-box: from 29.25% to 50.25%, a 21 pp gain (p < 0.001, chi2 = 45.95).

Brand trust (the fourth-ranked driver) and consistency (ranked sixth) also showed significant year-over-year improvements. Trust top-2-box rose from 21.25% (85/400) to 31.0% (124/400), a 9.75 pp gain (p < 0.001, chi2 = 26.50). Consistency top-2-box rose from 17.5% to 37.25%, a 19.75 pp gain (p < 0.001, chi2 = 52.62). These broad-based gains across both sensory and relational attributes indicate the improvement is not a narrow halo effect but a genuine shift in how consumers experience and perceive the brand.

All four attributes improved significantly at p < 0.001. Sweetness/flavor (3rd ranked driver) and appearance (7th ranked) also improved significantly; see appendix for full table.

Theme 3: Purchase Intent and Advocacy Have Shifted Materially Upward

Purchase intent positive scores nearly tripled year-over-year (16.75% to 40.75%), and the NPS distribution shifted meaningfully toward promoter territory — yet overall NPS remains at -35, confirming Acme's mid-journey brand status.

Q6: "How likely are you to purchase Acme Strawberries the next time you shop for fresh strawberries?" (Scale: 1 = Definitely will not purchase, 5 = Definitely will purchase)

Purchase intent shifted substantially from neutral to positive territory in 2026. The proportion of respondents indicating probable or definite future purchase (scores 4 or 5) nearly tripled, from 16.75% (67/400) in 2025 to 40.75% (163/400) in 2026. Simultaneously, combined negative intent (scores 1 or 2) fell from 20.0% (80/400) to 6.0% (24/400). The neutral segment (score 3) declined from 63.25% to 53.25%, indicating genuine conversion toward positive intent rather than a simple shift away from negative poles (p < 0.001, chi2 = 76.32).

Expected frequency warning: The chi-square test for Q6 × wave has min expected frequency = 0.5 (< 5), driven by only one total respondent selecting score 1. The practical conclusion is unaffected given the magnitude of the effect (chi2 = 76.32), but the precise p-value should be treated as approximate.

The Net Promoter Score (NPS) — measured on a 0–10 likelihood-to-recommend scale — showed the largest shift of any metric in the study. The NPS score distribution shifted strongly toward higher values in 2026 (p < 0.001, chi2 = 130.98, the single largest chi-square in the study). Score 10 respondents are 81.8% from 2026 (54 of 66); score 2 respondents are 79.4% from 2025 (27 of 34).

However, the overall NPS across both waves combined remains deeply negative:

  • Promoters (scores 9–10): 18.6% (149/800)
  • Detractors (scores 0–6): 53.8% (430/800)
  • Overall NPS: -35

This confirms Acme is in a mid-journey brand position: the trajectory is strongly positive, but the stock of satisfied advocates has not yet overcome the base of detractors accumulated over prior periods.

Theme 4: Acme Is Gaining Ground on Its Primary Competitor, Crimson Crown Berries

As Acme gained 13.5 pp in purchase rate (28.5% to 42.0%), Crimson Crown lost 13.0 pp (48.75% to 35.75%), and Acme's weekly buyers tripled from 3.0% to 9.75% — signaling both competitive switching and deepening loyalty.

The mirror-image movements in Acme's and Crimson Crown's purchase and preference metrics are among the most strategically significant patterns in this study.

Crimson Crown Berries — the category leader by both purchase rate and aided awareness at the time of the 2025 wave — lost purchase share in 2026 precisely as Acme gained it. Crimson Crown's purchase rate fell from 48.75% (195/400) to 35.75% (143/400), a decline of 13 percentage points (p < 0.001, chi2 = 13.33). In brand preference, Acme rose from 15.95% to 34.38%, surpassing Crimson Crown, which fell from 30.81% to 23.96%.

Beyond trial expansion, Acme's purchase frequency among all respondents also deepened materially. The weekly-or-more buyer segment tripled from 3.0% (12/400) to 9.75% (39/400). The combined high-frequency (often + very often) segment more than doubled, from 9.75% to 22.5% (p < 0.001, chi2 = 29.66). Importantly, overall category purchase frequency did not change significantly year-over-year (p = 0.143), supporting a share-of-wallet interpretation: Acme is earning more of the same category spend rather than benefiting from an expanding category.

The causal inference that Acme is directly recruiting buyers from Crimson Crown is plausible given the mirror-image share changes, but cannot be confirmed from cross-sectional wave data. A panel design tracking individual respondents across waves would be required to confirm individual switching behavior.

Theme 5: Current Brand Health Scores Remain Below Aspirational Benchmarks Despite Progress

The modal response on every key brand attribute is neutral (score 3), 58.25% of all respondents chose the purchase intent midpoint, and NPS is -35 — confirming a large unconverted middle segment that represents Acme's primary growth opportunity.

The year-over-year progress documented above must be understood in the context of where absolute ratings remain. Despite the directional improvements, the majority response on every key brand attribute remains the neutral midpoint (score 3 on the 5-point Likert scale):

  • Overall quality (Q5_01): Modal response = 3, chosen by 41.25% (330/800) of all respondents; top-2-box combined waves = 39.1% (313/800)
  • Taste fresh (Q5_02): Modal response = 3, chosen by 39.9% (319/800); top-2-box = 39.75% (318/800)
  • Brand I trust (Q5_10): Modal response = 3, chosen by 48.9% (391/800); top-2-box = 26.1% (209/800)

Similarly, the majority of all respondents (both waves combined) remain in neutral territory on purchase intent: 58.25% (466/800) chose the midpoint (score 3), and only 28.75% (230/800) indicated positive intent. The overall NPS of -35 (Promoters = 18.6%, Detractors = 53.8%) confirms the brand has not yet achieved the advocacy base expected of a market leader.

This neutral majority is not a sign of failure — the trajectory from 2025 to 2026 is unambiguously positive — but it does identify the primary opportunity: a large and demographically broad pool of category purchasers who know and use Acme but have not yet become committed buyers or advocates.

Theme 6: Demographic Variation Is Narrow — Acme's Performance Is Broad-Based

Only 26 of 191 chi-square tests reached significance at p < 0.05, and region produced zero significant results, confirming Acme's momentum is demographically broad-based.

Across 191 total chi-square tests conducted — spanning five demographic variables (wave year, age group, gender, region, and household income) and all question columns — only 26 tests (13.6%) reached statistical significance at p < 0.05. The vast majority of those 26 significant results reflect the wave (year) effect documented above, not demographic differences.

Region produced zero statistically significant results across all 19 tests. Age produced only 2 borderline significant results, both on minor metrics: aided awareness of Harvest Hill Berries (a minor competitor; p = 0.041) and sustainability attribute ratings (p = 0.039; ranked 10th in purchase intent importance). Gender produced only 1 significant result — shelf life ratings (p = 0.034) — but this test carries an expected frequency warning (min expected frequency = 0.73) that makes the result potentially unreliable.

The most notable demographic finding is an income-related pattern in overall quality ratings. Higher-income households ($100K+) show lower "Strongly agree" rates on quality (7.4%, 22/297) compared to mid-income ($50K–$99K: 12.9%, 40/310) and lower-income (Under $50K: 13.0%, 25/193) groups (p = 0.016, chi2 = 18.85). However, this test also carries an expected frequency warning (min = 3.14 < 5) and should be interpreted cautiously.

The broad-based nature of Acme's performance — consistent across age, gender, and region — supports mass-market positioning rather than demographic targeting for the primary marketing investment.

Key Drivers of Purchase Intent

Derived Importance Analysis: What Drives Acme Purchase Intent?

This section reports the results of a derived importance analysis — a regression-based method that identifies which brand attributes are most strongly associated with purchase intent. Two complementary metrics are reported for each attribute:

  • Total importance (Pearson r): The overall correlation between each attribute rating and purchase intent. A higher r indicates that respondents who rate Acme favorably on that attribute are more likely to intend to purchase. This captures the attribute's combined direct and indirect relationship with intent.
  • Unique importance (standardized beta): The attribute's independent contribution to predicting purchase intent after accounting for all other attributes in the regression model. A higher beta indicates the attribute adds predictive power beyond what other attributes already capture.
Source: derived_importance.csv. Analysis based on N = 800 respondents. NS = not statistically significant at p < 0.05.

Interpretation

Three sensory quality attributes dominate. Overall quality (r = 0.44, beta = 0.34), freshness (r = 0.39, beta = 0.30), and sweetness/flavor (r = 0.29, beta = 0.22) occupy the top three positions by both metrics. There is a substantial gap between the third-ranked attribute (sweetness/flavor beta = 0.22) and the fourth-ranked (trust beta = 0.14), suggesting these three form a distinct sensory quality cluster that drives purchase intent more powerfully than any other attribute category.

Brand trust has disproportionate unique importance. Trust ranks fourth overall (r = 0.21) but its standardized beta (0.14) is considerably higher relative to its correlation than consistency (r = 0.15, beta = 0.06) or value (r = 0.16, beta = 0.12). This means trust predicts purchase intent independently of the sensory cluster — it is not simply a proxy for quality perceptions but an additional, non-redundant driver.

Wide availability is effectively irrelevant to purchase intent. Availability has a near-zero and slightly negative beta (-0.0003) after controlling for other attributes, and its Pearson r (0.067) is not statistically significant at p < 0.05 (p = 0.058). This finding is specific to this screened sample of existing category purchasers, for whom availability is not the binding constraint. It should not be interpreted as availability being unimportant for non-purchasers or trial audiences.

Sustainability and packaging are low-priority commercial levers. Both are statistically significant at the bivariate level (p = 0.047 and p = 0.024, respectively) but have minimal unique contributions (betas of 0.04 and 0.05). They are unlikely to meaningfully move purchase intent even if substantially improved.

Methodological caution: The three top sensory attributes are likely intercorrelated (they share variance), which can cause instability in individual beta estimates through multicollinearity. The directional recommendation — invest in sensory quality signals — is robust. Precise beta values for attributes 1–3 should be interpreted as directional rather than exact. This analysis is correlational; it identifies attributes associated with higher purchase intent but does not establish causation.

5. Cross-Tabulation Analysis

Cross-tabulation (cross-tab) analysis examines whether response distributions on survey questions differ significantly across demographic groups. For each pairing of a demographic variable and a survey question, a chi-square test of independence was conducted. Only results that are statistically significant at p < 0.05 are reported here. A total of 191 chi-square tests were conducted; 26 reached significance at p < 0.05.

The overwhelming majority of significant results reflect the survey wave (year) effect — the year-over-year brand improvements documented in the Key Findings section. Traditional demographic variables (age, gender, and region) produced almost no significant differences on core brand metrics, underscoring that Acme's performance is broad-based.

Wave (Year) Effects — Statistically Significant Results

The wave variable (2025 vs. 2026) produced 21 of the 26 significant tests and drives the primary findings of this study.

Age Effects (Q14) — Statistically Significant Results

Age (collapsed into three bands: 18–34, 35–54, 55+) produced 2 statistically significant results out of 38 age-related tests, neither of which involves core Acme brand metrics.

No statistically significant age differences were found for Acme unaided or aided awareness, Acme purchase rate, any core Acme quality attribute (quality, freshness, trust), purchase intent, or NPS.

Gender Effects (Q15) — Statistically Significant Results

Regional Effects (Q16)

6. Conclusions

This section presents five evidence-based conclusions drawn from the Q1 2026 wave findings. Each conclusion is classified by the strength of its evidentiary basis: strong conclusions rest on statistically significant results across multiple metrics; moderate conclusions rest on significant results with caveats or interpretive steps that go beyond the observable data.

Conclusion 1: Acme Strawberries Has Achieved a Meaningful Brand Health Inflection Strong

The 2025-to-2026 improvements are not incremental — they represent a statistically significant, broad-based step-change across every primary brand metric tracked by this study.

Recommendation: Continue and accelerate the marketing and product initiatives that drove the 2025-to-2026 improvement. Identify which specific programs correlate with the timing of these gains to inform future investment allocation.

Conclusion 2: Acme Is Gaining Share at the Direct Expense of Crimson Crown Berries Moderate

Crimson Crown Berries — the category leader by purchase rate at the time of the 2025 survey — lost purchase share and preference simultaneously as Acme gained. The mirror-image movements are consistent with competitive switching, and Acme has taken the brand preference lead for the first time.

  • Crimson Crown purchase rate fell 13 pp (48.75% → 35.75%, p < 0.001) precisely as Acme rose 13.5 pp
  • Crimson Crown brand preference fell from 30.81% to 23.96%; Acme rose from 15.95% to 34.38%
  • Acme's high-frequency (often + very often) buyer segment more than doubled: 9.75% → 22.5% (p < 0.001)
  • Overall category purchase frequency did not change significantly (p = 0.143), supporting a share-of-wallet interpretation
Recommendation: Develop competitive messaging and retention strategies targeting Crimson Crown's remaining buyer base, emphasizing the sensory quality and freshness attributes where Acme has improved most sharply.
Caveat: The causal inference that Acme is directly recruiting buyers from Crimson Crown is plausible given the aggregate share shifts, but cannot be confirmed from cross-sectional wave data. Individual-level switching behavior would require a panel study.

Conclusion 3: Sensory Quality Is the Primary Commercial Lever; Brand Trust Has Disproportionate Independent Value Moderate

The derived importance analysis reveals a clear hierarchy of purchase intent drivers. Investments in sensory quality signaling will have the greatest commercial return. Brand trust has unique predictive power that exceeds what its correlation rank suggests.

  • Overall quality (r = 0.44, beta = 0.34) is the strongest driver by both total and unique importance — and top-2-box quality ratings more than doubled year-over-year
  • Freshness (r = 0.39, beta = 0.30) and sweetness/flavor (r = 0.29, beta = 0.22) complete a dominant top-three sensory cluster
  • Trust (r = 0.21, beta = 0.14) has a disproportionately high unique contribution — its beta is more than twice that of consistency (r = 0.15, beta = 0.06)
  • Wide availability has effectively zero unique contribution (beta = -0.0003) within this screened sample
Recommendation: Prioritize quality and freshness signaling in brand communications. Invest in trust-building activities (e.g., sourcing transparency, traceability, consistency messaging) given trust's independent predictive value. Deprioritize wide availability and packaging as purchase intent levers.

Conclusion 4: A Large Neutral Segment Represents Acme's Primary Growth Opportunity Strong

Despite significant directional progress, the majority of respondents remain neutral on purchase intent, and absolute brand health scores are centered at the scale midpoint.

  • 58.25% (466/800) of all respondents chose the purchase intent midpoint (score 3)
  • Only 28.75% (230/800) indicated positive intent (scores 4 or 5)
  • Trust top-2-box: 26.1% overall; quality top-2-box: 39.1% overall
  • NPS: Promoters = 18.6%, Detractors = 53.8%, NPS = -35
Recommendation: Target the neutral segment (score-3 purchase intent) as the highest-priority conversion audience. Design trial programs, sampling campaigns, and direct communications specifically to shift neutrals to probable purchasers.

Conclusion 5: Acme's Performance Is Broad-Based — Mass-Market Positioning Is Appropriate Moderate

The near-absence of statistically significant demographic variation across age, gender, and region indicates that Acme's growth trajectory is not driven by a narrow demographic segment.

  • Region: 0 of 19 regional tests significant at p < 0.05
  • Age: Only 2 borderline significant results, on minor metrics
  • Gender: Only 1 borderline result, with an expected frequency warning
  • Income: Most notable finding ($100K+ showing slightly lower Strongly agree rates on quality, p = 0.016) carries an expected frequency warning
Recommendation: Maintain broad-based mass-market positioning rather than demographic targeting for the primary marketing investment.

E. Data Limitations Reference

  1. Cross-sectional wave design: Year-over-year comparisons use different respondents in each wave. Individual-level switching behavior cannot be confirmed from aggregate share shifts.
  2. Chi-square expected frequency warnings: Q6 × year (min=0.5) and Q10 × year (min=3.5) have cells below the conventional threshold of 5. Results are practically unaffected given the magnitude of effects.
  3. Income × quality warning: Min expected frequency = 3.14; p-value of 0.016 may be unreliable.
  4. Gender × shelf life warning: Min expected frequency = 0.73; p-value of 0.034 is likely unreliable.
  5. Q11 missing data: 46 respondents (5.75%) did not answer brand preference; valid N = 754.
  6. Derived importance multicollinearity: Top three sensory attributes are likely intercorrelated; individual beta values should be treated as directional.
  7. Derived importance is correlational: Identifies associations with purchase intent; does not establish causation.
  8. Availability finding is sample-specific: Zero unique beta reflects a screened sample of existing category purchasers for whom availability is already resolved.
  9. Demographic frequency tables unavailable: Exact demographic composition (age, gender, state) cannot be reported from available files.
  10. Causal mechanisms for awareness growth are unobservable: Reasons for brand awareness gains cannot be determined from survey data.
