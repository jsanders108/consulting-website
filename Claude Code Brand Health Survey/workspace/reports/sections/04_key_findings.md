# Key Findings

The findings below are organized by theme, reflecting the most analytically coherent groupings of the data rather than question-by-question order. Statistical significance is assessed using chi-square tests of independence; a result is considered statistically significant when p < 0.05, meaning there is less than a 5% probability the observed difference is due to chance.

---

## Theme 1: Acme Strawberries Is on a Steep Upward Trajectory

**Acme's aided awareness surged 17.25 percentage points year-over-year and its purchase rate jumped 13.5 points, with brand preference flipping from second to first place.**

The most consequential finding of this study is the scale and breadth of Acme's year-over-year improvement. Brand awareness, purchase behavior, and brand preference all moved significantly in the same direction — and at the same time.

Aided brand awareness — recognition of Acme when shown a list of brands — grew from **65.25%** (261 of 400 respondents) in 2025 to **82.5%** (330 of 400) in 2026, a gain of **17.25 percentage points** (chi-square test, p < 0.001, chi2 = 29.95). This is the largest absolute awareness shift observed in the study. At the same time, unaided brand recall — the ability to name Acme spontaneously, without prompting — grew from **30.75%** (123/400) to **41.75%** (167/400), an **11 percentage point gain** (p = 0.002, chi2 = 10.00). The growth in unaided recall is particularly meaningful because it indicates Acme is becoming more top-of-mind, not merely better recognized when prompted.

Crucially, these awareness gains translated directly into commercial outcomes. Purchase rate rose from **28.5%** (114/400) to **42.0%** (168/400) — a **13.5 pp gain** (p < 0.001, chi2 = 15.38). Brand preference, measured by asking respondents which brand they would choose if all were equally priced, shifted even more dramatically: Acme rose from **15.95%** (59/370 valid respondents) to **34.38%** (132/384), surpassing Crimson Crown Berries to become the **top preferred brand** for the first time (p < 0.001, chi2 = 34.37).

> *Note: Q11 (brand preference) had 46 non-responses out of 800; per-wave denominators are 370 (2025) and 384 (2026).*

### Table 1: Acme Strawberries — Key Metrics Year-Over-Year

| Metric | 2025 | 2026 | Change | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Unaided brand awareness (Q1/Q2) | 30.75% (123/400) | 41.75% (167/400) | +11.0 pp | 0.002 |
| Aided brand awareness (Q3) | 65.25% (261/400) | 82.5% (330/400) | +17.25 pp | <0.001 |
| Purchase rate past 12 months (Q4) | 28.5% (114/400) | 42.0% (168/400) | +13.5 pp | <0.001 |
| Brand preference (Q11) | 15.95% (59/370) | 34.38% (132/384) | +18.43 pp | <0.001 |

> *Caution: The causal mechanisms driving awareness growth — such as earned media, distribution expansion, or advertising — cannot be determined from survey data alone. These metrics confirm that growth occurred; they do not explain why.*

## Theme 2: Perception Improvements Are Concentrated in Acme's Most Commercially Valuable Attributes

**Acme's sharpest perceptual gains occurred on the attributes that matter most to purchase intent — quality, freshness, consistency, and trust — all improving significantly year-over-year.**

Not all perceptual improvements are equally valuable. What makes Acme's 2026 gains particularly strategically significant is that the attributes that improved most sharply are precisely those with the strongest demonstrated links to purchase intent (see the Key Drivers of Purchase Intent section below).

Overall quality perception — the single strongest driver of purchase intent — more than doubled on top-2-box agreement (scores 4 or 5 on the 5-point Likert scale): from **25.75%** in 2025 to **52.5%** in 2026, a gain of **26.75 percentage points** (p < 0.001, chi2 = 76.61). Strongly agree scores (score 5 only) rose from 4.25% to 17.5%.

Freshness perception — the second strongest purchase intent driver — nearly doubled on top-2-box: from **29.25%** to **50.25%**, a **21 pp gain** (p < 0.001, chi2 = 45.95).

Brand trust (the fourth-ranked driver) and consistency (ranked sixth) also showed significant year-over-year improvements. Trust top-2-box rose from **21.25%** (85/400) to **31.0%** (124/400), a **9.75 pp gain** (p < 0.001, chi2 = 26.50). Consistency top-2-box rose from **17.5%** to **37.25%**, a **19.75 pp gain** (p < 0.001, chi2 = 52.62). These broad-based gains across both sensory and relational attributes indicate the improvement is not a narrow halo effect but a genuine shift in how consumers experience and perceive the brand.

### Table 2: Brand Attribute Perception — Top-2-Box (Scores 4+5) Year-Over-Year

| Attribute | 2025 Top-2-Box | 2026 Top-2-Box | Change | p-value | Purchase Intent Rank |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| High quality overall (Q5_01) | 25.75% | 52.5% | +26.75 pp | <0.001 | 1st |
| Taste fresh (Q5_02) | 29.25% | 50.25% | +21.0 pp | <0.001 | 2nd |
| Consistently good (Q5_06) | 17.5% | 37.25% | +19.75 pp | <0.001 | 6th |
| Brand I trust (Q5_10) | 21.25% | 31.0% | +9.75 pp | <0.001 | 4th |

> *All four attributes improved significantly at p < 0.001. Sweetness/flavor (3rd ranked driver) and appearance (7th ranked) also improved significantly; see Table in appendix.*

## Theme 3: Purchase Intent and Advocacy Have Shifted Materially Upward

**Purchase intent positive scores nearly tripled year-over-year (16.75% to 40.75%), and the NPS distribution shifted meaningfully toward promoter territory — yet overall NPS remains at -35, confirming Acme's mid-journey brand status.**

> Q6: "How likely are you to purchase Acme Strawberries the next time you shop for fresh strawberries?"
> (Scale: 1 = Definitely will not purchase, 5 = Definitely will purchase)

Purchase intent shifted substantially from neutral to positive territory in 2026. The proportion of respondents indicating probable or definite future purchase (scores 4 or 5) nearly tripled, from **16.75%** (67/400) in 2025 to **40.75%** (163/400) in 2026. Simultaneously, combined negative intent (scores 1 or 2) fell from **20.0%** (80/400) to **6.0%** (24/400). The neutral segment (score 3) declined from **63.25%** to **53.25%**, indicating genuine conversion toward positive intent rather than a simple shift away from negative poles (p < 0.001, chi2 = 76.32).

### Table 3: Purchase Intent Distribution Year-Over-Year (Q6)

| Response | 2025 | 2026 |
|---|---|---|
| 5 — Definitely will purchase | 0.0% (0/400) | 1.75% (7/400) |
| 4 — Probably will purchase | 16.75% (67/400) | 39.0% (156/400) |
| 3 — Neutral | 63.25% (253/400) | 53.25% (213/400) |
| 2 — Probably will not purchase | 19.75% (79/400) | 6.0% (24/400) |
| 1 — Definitely will not purchase | 0.25% (1/400) | 0.0% (0/400) |
| **Positive intent (4+5)** | **16.75%** | **40.75%** |
| **Negative intent (1+2)** | **20.0%** | **6.0%** |

> *Expected frequency warning: The chi-square test for Q6 × wave has min expected frequency = 0.5 (< 5), driven by only one total respondent selecting score 1. The practical conclusion is unaffected given the magnitude of the effect (chi2 = 76.32), but the precise p-value should be treated as approximate.*

The Net Promoter Score (NPS) — measured on a 0–10 likelihood-to-recommend scale — showed the largest shift of any metric in the study. The NPS score distribution shifted strongly toward higher values in 2026 (p < 0.001, chi2 = 130.98, the single largest chi-square in the study). Score 10 respondents are **81.8%** from 2026 (54 of 66); score 2 respondents are **79.4%** from 2025 (27 of 34).

However, the overall NPS across both waves combined remains deeply negative:

- **Promoters** (scores 9–10): **18.6%** (149/800)
- **Detractors** (scores 0–6): **53.8%** (430/800)
- **Overall NPS: -35**

This confirms Acme is in a mid-journey brand position: the trajectory is strongly positive, but the stock of satisfied advocates has not yet overcome the base of detractors accumulated over prior periods.

> *NPS expected frequency note: The Q10 NPS × wave chi-square test has min expected frequency = 3.5 (< 5), reflecting the 11-point scale across two waves. At chi2 = 130.98, the result is unambiguous.*

## Theme 4: Acme Is Gaining Ground on Its Primary Competitor, Crimson Crown Berries

**As Acme gained 13.5 pp in purchase rate (28.5% to 42.0%), Crimson Crown lost 13.0 pp (48.75% to 35.75%), and Acme's weekly buyers tripled from 3.0% to 9.75% — signaling both competitive switching and deepening loyalty.**

The mirror-image movements in Acme's and Crimson Crown's purchase and preference metrics are among the most strategically significant patterns in this study.

Crimson Crown Berries — the category leader by both purchase rate and aided awareness at the time of the 2025 wave — lost purchase share in 2026 precisely as Acme gained it. Crimson Crown's purchase rate fell from **48.75%** (195/400) to **35.75%** (143/400), a decline of **13 percentage points** (p < 0.001, chi2 = 13.33). In brand preference, Acme rose from **15.95%** to **34.38%**, surpassing Crimson Crown, which fell from **30.81%** to **23.96%**.

Beyond trial expansion, Acme's purchase frequency among all respondents also deepened materially. The weekly-or-more buyer segment tripled from **3.0%** (12/400) to **9.75%** (39/400). The combined high-frequency (often + very often) segment more than doubled, from **9.75%** to **22.5%** (p < 0.001, chi2 = 29.66). Importantly, overall category purchase frequency did not change significantly year-over-year (p = 0.143), supporting a share-of-wallet interpretation: Acme is earning more of the same category spend rather than benefiting from an expanding category.

### Table 4: Purchase Rate Comparison — Acme vs. Crimson Crown Year-Over-Year (Q4)

| Brand | 2025 Purchase Rate | 2026 Purchase Rate | Change | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Acme Strawberries | 28.5% (114/400) | 42.0% (168/400) | +13.5 pp | <0.001 |
| Crimson Crown Berries | 48.75% (195/400) | 35.75% (143/400) | -13.0 pp | <0.001 |

> *Caution: The causal inference that Acme is directly recruiting buyers from Crimson Crown is plausible given the mirror-image share changes, but cannot be confirmed from cross-sectional wave data. A panel design tracking individual respondents across waves would be required to confirm individual switching behavior.*

## Theme 5: Current Brand Health Scores Remain Below Aspirational Benchmarks Despite Progress

**The modal response on every key brand attribute is neutral (score 3), 58.25% of all respondents chose the purchase intent midpoint, and NPS is -35 — confirming a large unconverted middle segment that represents Acme's primary growth opportunity.**

The year-over-year progress documented above must be understood in the context of where absolute ratings remain. Despite the directional improvements, the majority response on every key brand attribute remains the neutral midpoint (score 3 on the 5-point Likert scale):

- **Overall quality (Q5_01):** Modal response = 3, chosen by **41.25%** (330/800) of all respondents; top-2-box combined waves = **39.1%** (313/800)
- **Taste fresh (Q5_02):** Modal response = 3, chosen by **39.9%** (319/800); top-2-box = **39.75%** (318/800)
- **Brand I trust (Q5_10):** Modal response = 3, chosen by **48.9%** (391/800); top-2-box = **26.1%** (209/800)

Similarly, the majority of all respondents (both waves combined) remain in neutral territory on purchase intent: **58.25%** (466/800) chose the midpoint (score 3), and only **28.75%** (230/800) indicated positive intent. The overall NPS of **-35** (Promoters = 18.6%, Detractors = 53.8%) confirms the brand has not yet achieved the advocacy base expected of a market leader.

This neutral majority is not a sign of failure — the trajectory from 2025 to 2026 is unambiguously positive — but it does identify the primary opportunity: a large and demographically broad pool of category purchasers who know and use Acme but have not yet become committed buyers or advocates.

## Theme 6: Demographic Variation Is Narrow — Acme's Performance Is Broad-Based

**Only 26 of 191 chi-square tests reached significance at p < 0.05, and region produced zero significant results, confirming Acme's momentum is demographically broad-based.**

Across 191 total chi-square tests conducted — spanning five demographic variables (wave year, age group, gender, region, and household income) and all question columns — only **26 tests** (13.6%) reached statistical significance at p < 0.05. The vast majority of those 26 significant results reflect the wave (year) effect documented above, not demographic differences.

Region produced **zero** statistically significant results across all 19 tests. Age produced only **2** borderline significant results, both on minor metrics: aided awareness of Harvest Hill Berries (a minor competitor; p = 0.041) and sustainability attribute ratings (p = 0.039; ranked 10th in purchase intent importance). Gender produced only **1** significant result — shelf life ratings (p = 0.034) — but this test carries an expected frequency warning (min expected frequency = 0.73) that makes the result potentially unreliable.

The most notable demographic finding is an income-related pattern in overall quality ratings. Higher-income households ($100K+) show lower "Strongly agree" rates on quality (**7.4%**, 22/297) compared to mid-income ($50K–$99K: **12.9%**, 40/310) and lower-income (Under $50K: **13.0%**, 25/193) groups (p = 0.016, chi2 = 18.85). However, this test also carries an expected frequency warning (min = 3.14 < 5) and should be interpreted cautiously.

> *The broad-based nature of Acme's performance — consistent across age, gender, and region — supports mass-market positioning rather than demographic targeting for the primary marketing investment.*

## Key Drivers of Purchase Intent

### Derived Importance Analysis: What Drives Acme Purchase Intent?

This section reports the results of a derived importance analysis — a regression-based method that identifies which brand attributes are most strongly associated with purchase intent. Two complementary metrics are reported for each attribute:

- **Total importance (Pearson r):** The overall correlation between each attribute rating and purchase intent. A higher r indicates that respondents who rate Acme favorably on that attribute are more likely to intend to purchase. This captures the attribute's combined direct and indirect relationship with intent.

- **Unique importance (standardized beta):** The attribute's independent contribution to predicting purchase intent after accounting for all other attributes in the regression model. A higher beta indicates the attribute adds predictive power beyond what other attributes already capture.

These metrics together reveal both which attributes matter most overall and which ones are non-redundant (i.e., not fully explained by other attributes).

### Table 5: Derived Importance — Brand Attribute Drivers of Purchase Intent (Q5 → Q6)

| Rank | Attribute | Pearson r (Total Importance) | Standardized Beta (Unique Importance) | Significance (p-value) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | High quality overall | **0.4379** | **0.3358** | < 0.001 |
| 2 | Taste fresh | **0.3857** | **0.3003** | < 0.001 |
| 3 | Excellent sweetness/flavor | **0.2886** | **0.2159** | < 0.001 |
| 4 | Brand I trust | **0.2144** | **0.1361** | < 0.001 |
| 5 | Good value for price | **0.1552** | **0.1208** | < 0.001 |
| 6 | Consistently good | **0.1523** | **0.0638** | < 0.001 |
| 7 | Appealing appearance | **0.1455** | **0.0575** | < 0.001 |
| 8 | Longer shelf life than competitors | **0.1044** | **0.0744** | 0.003 |
| 9 | Attractive packaging | **0.0796** | **0.0485** | 0.024 |
| 10 | Committed to sustainability | **0.0702** | **0.0416** | 0.047 |
| 11 | Widely available | 0.0672 | -0.0003 | 0.058 (not significant) |

*Source: derived_importance.csv. Analysis based on N = 800 respondents.*

### Interpretation

**Three sensory quality attributes dominate.** Overall quality (r = 0.44, beta = 0.34), freshness (r = 0.39, beta = 0.30), and sweetness/flavor (r = 0.29, beta = 0.22) occupy the top three positions by both metrics. There is a substantial gap between the third-ranked attribute (sweetness/flavor beta = 0.22) and the fourth-ranked (trust beta = 0.14), suggesting these three form a distinct sensory quality cluster that drives purchase intent more powerfully than any other attribute category.

**Brand trust has disproportionate unique importance.** Trust ranks fourth overall (r = 0.21) but its standardized beta (0.14) is considerably higher relative to its correlation than consistency (r = 0.15, beta = 0.06) or value (r = 0.16, beta = 0.12). This means trust predicts purchase intent independently of the sensory cluster — it is not simply a proxy for quality perceptions but an additional, non-redundant driver.

**Wide availability is effectively irrelevant to purchase intent.** Availability has a near-zero and slightly negative beta (-0.0003) after controlling for other attributes, and its Pearson r (0.067) is not statistically significant at p < 0.05 (p = 0.058). This finding is specific to this screened sample of existing category purchasers, for whom availability is not the binding constraint. It should not be interpreted as availability being unimportant for non-purchasers or trial audiences.

**Sustainability and packaging are low-priority commercial levers.** Both are statistically significant at the bivariate level (p = 0.047 and p = 0.024, respectively) but have minimal unique contributions (betas of 0.04 and 0.05). They are unlikely to meaningfully move purchase intent even if substantially improved.

> *Methodological caution: The three top sensory attributes are likely intercorrelated (they share variance), which can cause instability in individual beta estimates through multicollinearity. The directional recommendation — invest in sensory quality signals — is robust. Precise beta values for attributes 1–3 should be interpreted as directional rather than exact. This analysis is correlational; it identifies attributes associated with higher purchase intent but does not establish causation.*
