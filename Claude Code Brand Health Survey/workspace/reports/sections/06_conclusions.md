# Conclusions

This section presents five evidence-based conclusions drawn from the Q1 2026 wave findings. Each conclusion is classified by the strength of its evidentiary basis: **strong** conclusions rest on statistically significant results across multiple metrics; **moderate** conclusions rest on significant results with caveats or interpretive steps that go beyond the observable data.

The study objectives — measuring brand awareness, perceptions, purchase behavior, and competitive position — are addressed across these five conclusions.

---

## Conclusion 1: Acme Strawberries Has Achieved a Meaningful Brand Health Inflection (Strong)

The 2025-to-2026 improvements are not incremental — they represent a statistically significant, broad-based step-change across every primary brand metric tracked by this study.

**Supporting evidence:**

| Metric | 2025 | 2026 | Change | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Aided brand awareness | 65.25% | 82.5% | +17.25 pp | p < 0.001 |
| Unaided brand recall | 30.75% | 41.75% | +11.0 pp | p = 0.002 |
| Purchase rate (past 12 months) | 28.5% | 42.0% | +13.5 pp | p < 0.001 |
| Brand preference | 15.95% | 34.38% | +18.43 pp | p < 0.001 |
| Positive purchase intent | 16.75% | 40.75% | +24.0 pp | p < 0.001 |

**Recommendation:** Continue and accelerate the marketing and product initiatives that drove the 2025-to-2026 improvement. Identify which specific programs correlate with the timing of these gains to inform future investment allocation. The pace of improvement across all six primary metrics in a single year is exceptional; understanding the drivers of this inflection is a strategic priority.

---

## Conclusion 2: Acme Is Gaining Share at the Direct Expense of Crimson Crown Berries (Moderate)

Crimson Crown Berries — the category leader by purchase rate at the time of the 2025 survey — lost purchase share and preference simultaneously as Acme gained. The mirror-image movements are consistent with competitive switching, and Acme has taken the brand preference lead for the first time.

**Supporting evidence:**

- Crimson Crown purchase rate fell **13 pp** (48.75% → 35.75%, p < 0.001) precisely as Acme rose **13.5 pp**
- Crimson Crown brand preference fell from **30.81%** to **23.96%**; Acme rose from **15.95%** to **34.38%**
- Acme's high-frequency (often + very often) buyer segment more than doubled: **9.75% → 22.5%** (p < 0.001)
- Overall category purchase frequency did not change significantly (p = 0.143), supporting a share-of-wallet interpretation

**Recommendation:** Develop competitive messaging and retention strategies targeting Crimson Crown's remaining buyer base, emphasizing the sensory quality and freshness attributes where Acme has improved most sharply. Monitor competitor response — a 13 pp purchase rate loss and preference loss of this scale will likely prompt a strategic reaction from Crimson Crown.

> *Caveat: The causal inference that Acme is directly recruiting buyers from Crimson Crown is plausible given the aggregate share shifts, but cannot be confirmed from cross-sectional wave data. Individual-level switching behavior would require a panel study.*

## Conclusion 3: Sensory Quality Is the Primary Commercial Lever; Brand Trust Has Disproportionate Independent Value (Moderate)

The derived importance analysis reveals a clear hierarchy of purchase intent drivers. Investments in sensory quality signaling will have the greatest commercial return. Brand trust has unique predictive power that exceeds what its correlation rank suggests, making it a strategic priority beyond its position in the overall ranking.

**Supporting evidence:**

- Overall quality (r = **0.44**, beta = **0.34**) is the strongest driver of purchase intent by both total and unique importance — and top-2-box quality ratings more than doubled year-over-year
- Freshness (r = **0.39**, beta = **0.30**) and sweetness/flavor (r = **0.29**, beta = **0.22**) complete a dominant top-three sensory cluster
- Trust (r = **0.21**, beta = **0.14**) has a disproportionately high unique contribution — its beta is more than twice that of consistency (r = **0.15**, beta = **0.06**), indicating trust predicts intent independently of sensory quality
- Wide availability has effectively zero unique contribution (beta = **-0.0003**) within this screened sample

**Recommendation:** Prioritize quality and freshness signaling in brand communications — these are both the top drivers and the attributes where Acme has already made the greatest gains. Invest in trust-building activities (e.g., sourcing transparency, traceability, consistency messaging) given trust's independent predictive value. Deprioritize wide availability and packaging as purchase intent levers for this category-active audience.

> *Caveat: The top three sensory attributes are likely intercorrelated, which can cause multicollinearity in the regression model. The directional ranking is robust; precise beta values should be treated as directional indicators rather than exact magnitudes.*

## Conclusion 4: A Large Neutral Segment Represents Acme's Primary Growth Opportunity (Strong)

Despite significant directional progress, the majority of respondents remain neutral on purchase intent, and absolute brand health scores are centered at the scale midpoint. The large neutral segment is Acme's most accessible conversion target.

**Supporting evidence:**

- **58.25%** (466/800) of all respondents chose the purchase intent midpoint (score 3) across both waves combined
- Only **28.75%** (230/800) indicated positive intent (scores 4 or 5)
- Trust top-2-box: **26.1%** overall; quality top-2-box: **39.1%** overall
- NPS: Promoters = **18.6%**, Detractors = **53.8%**, **NPS = -35**

**Recommendation:** Target the neutral segment (score-3 purchase intent) as the highest-priority conversion audience. Design trial programs, sampling campaigns, and direct communications specifically to shift neutrals to probable purchasers, leveraging the sensory quality and freshness improvements already achieved. The NPS of -35 indicates that improving advocacy will require sustained investment rather than a single campaign; focus first on converting neutrals to purchasers, then on converting purchasers to advocates.

---

## Conclusion 5: Acme's Performance Is Broad-Based — Mass-Market Positioning Is Appropriate (Moderate)

The near-absence of statistically significant demographic variation across age, gender, and region indicates that Acme's growth trajectory is not driven by a narrow demographic segment. The brand's current positioning is reaching across the market, and there is no evidence that a pivot to demographic targeting would outperform broad-market investment.

**Supporting evidence:**

- Region: **0 of 19** regional tests significant at p < 0.05
- Age: Only **2 borderline significant** results, on minor metrics (Harvest Hill competitor awareness and sustainability attribute — both peripherally related to Acme strategy)
- Gender: Only **1 borderline result**, with an expected frequency warning that may render it unreliable
- Income: The most notable finding ($100K+ showing slightly lower Strongly agree rates on quality, p = 0.016) carries an expected frequency warning and warrants monitoring rather than immediate strategy response

**Recommendation:** Maintain broad-based mass-market positioning rather than demographic targeting for the primary marketing investment. The one exception worth monitoring is the income-quality perception gap — if higher-income households are the intended premium audience, understanding the source of their lower quality conviction (product experience, competitive alternatives, or unmet expectations) would merit qualitative follow-up.
