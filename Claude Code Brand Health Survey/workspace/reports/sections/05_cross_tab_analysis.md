# Cross-Tabulation Analysis

Cross-tabulation (cross-tab) analysis examines whether response distributions on survey questions differ significantly across demographic groups. For each pairing of a demographic variable and a survey question, a chi-square test of independence was conducted. Only results that are statistically significant at p < 0.05 are reported here. A total of **191 chi-square tests** were conducted; **26 reached significance** at p < 0.05.

The overwhelming majority of significant results reflect the survey wave (year) effect — the year-over-year brand improvements documented in the Key Findings section. Traditional demographic variables (age, gender, and region) produced almost no significant differences on core brand metrics, underscoring that Acme's performance is broad-based.

## Age Effects (Q14) — Statistically Significant Results

Age (collapsed into three bands: 18–34, 35–54, 55+) produced **2 statistically significant results** out of 38 age-related tests, neither of which involves core Acme brand metrics.

### Table 2: Significant Age Cross-Tabulations

| Question | Chi-Square | p-value | Direction | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Q3 — Aided Harvest Hill Berries awareness | 6.38 | 0.041 | Varies by age group | Minor competitor; limited Acme strategic relevance |
| Q5_08 — Committed to sustainability | 16.26 | 0.039 | Sustainability perception varies by age | Borderline significance; min expected freq = 5.73; sustainability is 10th-ranked driver |

No statistically significant age differences were found for Acme unaided or aided awareness, Acme purchase rate, any core Acme quality attribute (quality, freshness, trust), purchase intent, or NPS.

## Gender Effects (Q15) — Statistically Significant Results

Gender (collapsed to Male / Female / Other) produced **1 statistically significant result** out of 38 gender-related tests.

### Table 3: Significant Gender Cross-Tabulations

| Question | Chi-Square | p-value | Direction | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Q5_11 — Longer shelf life than competitors | 16.66 | 0.034 | Shelf life perception varies by gender | **Expected frequency warning: min expected freq = 0.73 (< 5). This result may be unreliable.** Shelf life is the 8th-ranked purchase intent driver. |

No statistically significant gender differences were found for awareness, purchase behavior, purchase intent, NPS, or brand preference.

## Regional Effects (Q16) — Statistically Significant Results

Region (Northeast/Midwest, South, West) produced **zero statistically significant results** across all 19 regional cross-tabulations tested. Acme's brand performance is geographically uniform across the sampled population.

## Income Effects (Q17) — Statistically Significant Results

Household income (three bands: $100K+, $50K–$99K, Under $50K) produced **2 statistically significant results** out of 38 income-related tests.

### Table 4: Significant Income Cross-Tabulations

| Question | Chi-Square | p-value | Direction | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Q5_01 — High quality overall | 18.85 | 0.016 | $100K+ lower Strongly agree (7.4%) vs. $50K–$99K (12.9%) and Under $50K (13.0%) | **Expected frequency warning: min expected freq = 3.14 (< 5). Treat with caution.** |
| Q7 — Category purchase frequency | 17.54 | 0.025 | Category frequency varies by income group | Higher-income groups may purchase strawberries at different frequencies |

> *The income × quality finding has been corrected from an initial methodological framing error (row percentages vs. within-group rates). The direction ($100K+ have lower Strongly agree rates) is preserved in the corrected analysis; within-group Strongly agree rates are: $100K+ = 7.41% (22/297), $50K–$99K = 12.90% (40/310), Under $50K = 12.95% (25/193). The expected frequency warning means the p-value of 0.016 may be unreliable.*
