# Respondent Profile

## Overview

The study includes 800 respondents screened as fresh strawberry purchasers within the past six months. The sample is split evenly across two survey waves (400 per wave). All respondents answered the screener criterion, confirming active category participation. Demographic breakdowns below reflect the information available from the data files produced.

## Age (Q14)

Standalone frequency data for age is not available from the files produced. For cross-tabulation analysis, age was collapsed into three bands:

- **18–34** (combining 18–24 and 25–34 year-olds)
- **35–54** (combining 35–44 and 45–54 year-olds)
- **55+** (combining 55–64 and 65 or older)

No statistically significant age-related differences were found on core brand metrics (awareness, purchase behavior, or brand attribute evaluations). Two borderline exceptions were observed: age versus Harvest Hill Berries aided awareness (p = 0.041) and age versus the sustainability attribute (p = 0.039); see Section 05 for details.

---

## Gender (Q15)

Standalone frequency data for gender is not available from the files produced. For cross-tabulation, gender was collapsed to three groups: Male, Female, and Other (combining Non-binary, Prefer not to say, and Other). No statistically significant gender effects were found on awareness, purchase, or brand evaluation metrics, with one borderline exception — gender versus the shelf life attribute (p = 0.034, with an expected frequency warning indicating this result may be unreliable).

---

## State / Region (Q16)

Standalone frequency data for state is not available from the files produced. For cross-tabulation, states were grouped into three regions: Northeast/Midwest, South, and West. Zero statistically significant regional differences were found across all 19 regional cross-tabulations tested (all p > 0.05), confirming that Acme's brand performance is geographically broad-based.
