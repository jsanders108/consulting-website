# Methodology

## Study Design

The ACME Strawberries Brand Health Tracking Survey is a two-wave cross-sectional study designed to measure brand awareness, perceptions, purchase behavior, and competitive position for Acme Strawberries against six competitors in the fresh strawberry market. The six competitor brands tracked are: Crimson Crown Berries, Sunrise Berries, Red Valley Organics, FreshPick Strawberries, Golden Meadow Berries, and Harvest Hill Berries.

## Sample

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Original sample size | 800 respondents |
| Cleaned sample size | 800 respondents |
| Data retention rate | 100% |
| Wave 1 (2025) | 400 respondents |
| Wave 2 (2026) | 400 respondents |
| Screener criterion | Purchased fresh strawberries in the past 6 months |

The two waves are equal in size by design, providing balanced year-over-year comparisons. All wave-level statistics use 400 as the denominator unless otherwise noted.

## Data Collection

Respondents were screened for fresh strawberry category purchasers within the past six months. The survey instrument collected unaided and aided brand awareness, past-year purchase behavior across seven brands, brand attribute evaluations (11 attributes on a 5-point Likert scale), purchase intent, category and brand purchase frequency, most recent brand purchased, Net Promoter Score (0–10 scale), brand preference, and four demographic variables (age, gender, state, household income).

## Analytical Methods

Three categories of statistical analysis were conducted:

**Frequency analysis:** Response distributions (counts and percentages) were computed for all survey questions. Valid N is reported for each question; missing data is noted where applicable.

**Cross-tabulation and chi-square testing:** Cross-tabulations were produced for all question columns by five demographic variables: survey wave (year), age group (Q14), gender (Q15), state/region (Q16), and household income (Q17). A total of **191 chi-square tests of independence** were conducted. Statistical significance was evaluated at the conventional alpha = 0.05 threshold. Chi-square tests assess whether the distribution of responses on a question differs significantly across demographic groups; a statistically significant result (p < 0.05) indicates the observed difference is unlikely to be due to chance alone.

**Derived importance analysis (regression-based):** Pearson correlation coefficients (r) and standardized regression beta coefficients were computed between each of the 11 Q5 brand attribute ratings and the Q6 purchase intent score, across all 800 respondents. Pearson r captures the overall (total) relationship between each attribute and purchase intent. Standardized beta coefficients, derived from a multiple regression model, capture each attribute's unique contribution to purchase intent after controlling for all other attributes. This method, known as derived importance analysis, identifies which attributes are most actionable for influencing purchase intent.

## Data Quality Notes

No respondents were excluded during data cleaning; the original and cleaned sample sizes are identical (N = 800). However, several data quality considerations apply to this report:

- **Missing data on Q11 (brand preference):** 46 of 800 respondents (5.75%) did not answer Q11, reducing the valid N to 754. Per-wave denominators for Q11 are 370 (2025) and 384 (2026).
- **Chi-square expected frequency warnings:** Two significance tests have expected cell frequencies below the conventional threshold of 5 — the Q6 purchase intent × wave test (min expected frequency = 0.5, driven by only one respondent selecting score 1) and the Q10 NPS × wave test (min expected frequency = 3.5, due to the 11-point scale across two waves). Both results are overwhelming in magnitude (chi-square = 76.3 and 130.976, respectively) and the practical conclusions are unaffected, but precise p-values should be treated as approximate.
- **Additional assumption warnings:** The income × quality cross-tab (p = 0.016, min expected frequency = 3.14) and the gender × shelf life cross-tab (p = 0.034, min expected frequency = 0.73) carry expected frequency warnings and should be interpreted with caution.
- **Demographic frequency tables:** Standalone frequency distributions for age (Q14), gender (Q15), and state (Q16) were not produced by the data processor. Exact demographic composition of the sample cannot be reported for these variables. Income group totals were derived from banner table column sums.
