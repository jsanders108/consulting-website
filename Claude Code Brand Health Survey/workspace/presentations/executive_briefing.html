Methodology & Data Notes

191

chi-square significance tests | N=800 | Two-wave cross-sectional design

  • Design: Two-wave cross-sectional (not a panel). 400 respondents per wave. Year-over-year comparisons reflect aggregate wave differences, not individual-level change.
  • Derived importance: Pearson r and standardized beta coefficients correlate 11 Q5 attributes with Q6 purchase intent across all 800 respondents. Top sensory attributes are likely intercorrelated; individual beta estimates may show instability from multicollinearity.
  • Brand preference: Valid n=754 (46 non-respondents). Per-wave denominators: 2025=370, 2026=384.
  • Expected frequency warnings: Purchase intent x year and NPS x year cross-tabs carry warnings due to sparse extreme-score cells. Conclusions are unaffected given chi-square magnitudes (76.32 and 130.98).
  • Income x quality: Significant (chi2=18.85, p=0.016) but expected frequency warning (min expected cell=3.14 < 5). Treat with caution.
