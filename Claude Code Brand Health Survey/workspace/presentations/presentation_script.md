# Presentation Script: ACME Strawberries Brand Health Tracking Survey — Q1 2026 Wave

**Total slides:** 10
**Narrative theme:** Acme Strawberries earned the #1 preference position in 2026 through genuine quality gains on the attributes that convert buyers — and the data points to one specific lever that can lock in that lead: trust.
**Target audience:** Acme Strawberries executive leadership

---

## Slide 1: Title

**Type:** Title

**Title:** Earned, Not Given

**Headline Stat:** For the first time, more buyers prefer Acme Strawberries than any other brand in the market.

**Narrative:** One year ago, Acme was the second-choice brand in a market dominated by Crimson Crown Berries. Today, it holds the #1 preference position — not because of a survey anomaly, but because it improved on exactly the attributes that drive buyers to purchase. This briefing tells that story, and identifies what must happen next to hold the lead.

**Chart Specification:** None — text and branding only

**Talking Points:**
- Q1 2026 Wave brand health tracking study. N=800 fresh strawberry purchasers in the past 6 months. Two-wave design: 400 respondents in 2025, 400 in 2026.
- Seven brands measured: Acme Strawberries, Crimson Crown Berries, Sunrise Berries, Red Valley Organics, FreshPick Strawberries, Golden Meadow Berries, Harvest Hill Berries.
- The hook: Acme overtook the category leader in a single year. What drove it, and what comes next, is what this briefing answers.

## Slide 2: Context

**Type:** Key Metric

**Title:** Who We Surveyed

**Headline Stat:** 800 respondents — 400 in 2025, 400 in 2026

**Narrative:** All respondents were screened as fresh strawberry purchasers in the past six months, giving us a picture of the active category buyer. The two equal waves allow direct year-over-year comparison on every metric. 191 chi-square significance tests were conducted; findings presented here all clear the p<0.05 threshold unless noted.

**Chart Specification:** None — text layout only. Display four key context points:
1. N=800 total (400 per wave)
2. Screen: fresh strawberry purchasers in past 6 months
3. 7 brands evaluated
4. 191 significance tests conducted across 5 demographic variables

**Talking Points:**
- Sample is split evenly by design — all year-over-year comparisons use 400 as the denominator per wave.
- Demographic profile: Age, gender, state/region, income tracked. Region produced zero statistically significant results across all tests — Acme's performance is geographically broad-based.
- Virtually no significant age or gender differences in core brand metrics, which means these are market-wide findings, not niche-segment stories.

## Slide 3: Comparison

**Type:** Comparison

**Title:** The Preference Flip: Acme Now Leads the Market

**Headline Stat:** Acme brand preference: 15.95% in 2025 → 34.38% in 2026

**Narrative:** In a single year, Acme Strawberries went from second place to first in brand preference among active category buyers. Acme's preference share more than doubled while Crimson Crown Berries — the prior leader — fell from 30.81% to 23.96%. This is not a marginal shift: the gap between them flipped from Crimson Crown leading by 15 points to Acme leading by more than 10 points.

**Chart Specification:** Grouped bar chart (2025 vs 2026) from `workspace/analysis/banner_tables/banner_year_Q11_brand_preference.csv`. Plot each brand's within-wave preference share for both years. Highlight Acme Strawberries and Crimson Crown Berries bars. Key values to label:
- Acme Strawberries: 2025 = 59/370 = 15.95%, 2026 = 132/384 = 34.38%
- Crimson Crown Berries: 2025 = 114/370 = 30.81%, 2026 = 92/384 = 23.96%
- Valid n = 754 (46 non-respondents excluded from Q11 denominators)

**Talking Points:**
- Acme's preference nearly doubled in a single wave. Crimson Crown's fell by a quarter.
- Purchase rates moved in lockstep — and in opposite directions. Acme purchase rate rose from 28.5% (114/400) to 42.0% (168/400), a gain of 13.5 percentage points. Crimson Crown purchase rate fell from 48.75% (195/400) to 35.75% (143/400), a decline of 13.0 pp. Source: `workspace/analysis/banner_tables/banner_year_Q4_purchased_Acme_Strawberries.csv`; `banner_year_Q4_purchased_Crimson_Crown_Berries.csv`.
- The mirror-image movement in purchase rates is consistent with direct competitive switching — buyers shifting wallet share from Crimson Crown to Acme.
- Aided awareness also rose sharply: 65.25% in 2025 to 82.5% in 2026 (+17.25 pp, chi2=29.95, p<0.001). Unaided recall rose from 30.75% to 41.75% (+11 pp, chi2=10.00, p=0.002). The brand is growing in both prompted and unprompted recognition.
- All three metrics — awareness, purchase, and preference — moved significantly in the same direction. This is a broad-based momentum story, not a single-metric artifact.

## Slide 4: Insight

**Type:** Insight

**Title:** Acme Improved on the Attributes That Convert Buyers

**Headline Stat:** Quality top-2-box doubled: 25.75% → 52.5% (chi2=76.61, p<0.001)

**Narrative:** Derived importance analysis reveals that the top two drivers of purchase intent are overall quality (Pearson r=0.44, beta=0.34) and freshness (r=0.39, beta=0.30). Acme's largest year-over-year perceptual gains occurred on exactly these two attributes — quality perception more than doubled, and freshness nearly doubled. The brand did not improve randomly; it improved where it counted most.

**Chart Specification:** Two-panel comparison layout. Source files: `workspace/analysis/banner_tables/banner_year_Q5_01_quality_overall.csv` and `workspace/analysis/banner_tables/banner_year_Q5_02_freshness.csv`. For each attribute, display a stacked bar or side-by-side bar showing top-2-box (scores 4+5) for 2025 vs. 2026:
- Quality overall: 2025 = (86+17)/400 = 25.75%, 2026 = (140+70)/400 = 52.5%
- Freshness: 2025 = (103+14)/400 = 29.25%, 2026 = (154+47)/400 = 50.25%
Label both bars clearly. Include a small sidebar or annotation showing derived importance rankings from `workspace/analysis/derived_importance.csv`: Quality #1 (r=0.44), Freshness #2 (r=0.39).

**Talking Points:**
- Purchase intent is driven primarily by three sensory quality attributes: quality overall, freshness, and sweetness/flavor (r=0.29, beta=0.22). Acme improved significantly on all three.
- Quality top-2-box more than doubled in a single year — from 1 in 4 agreeing to more than 1 in 2 (25.75% to 52.5%). Chi2=76.61, p<0.001.
- Freshness top-2-box rose from 29.25% to 50.25% — nearly doubling (+21 pp). Chi2=45.95, p<0.001.
- Consistency (ranked 6th in importance) and trust (ranked 4th) also improved significantly: Consistency top-2-box rose from 17.5% to 37.25% (chi2=52.62, p<0.001). Trust top-2-box rose from 21.25% to 31.0% (chi2=26.50, p<0.001).
- The pattern is analytically important: Acme did not get a generic "new and improved" halo across the board. Its biggest gains landed on its most commercially valuable attributes.
- Source: `workspace/analysis/derived_importance.csv`; `workspace/analysis/significance_results.csv`.

## Slide 5: Distribution

8. Longer shelf life: r=0.1044, beta=0.0744

**Talking Points:**

This means trust carries independent predictive power for purchase intent beyond what quality and freshness already capture. This will matter on the next slide.

## Slide 6: Key Metric

**Type:** Key Metric

**Title:** The Conversion Gap: 58% Are Still Watching

**Headline Stat:** 58.25% of all respondents chose the purchase intent midpoint (neutral)

**Narrative:** Despite the dramatic year-over-year progress, the majority of active category buyers remain uncommitted to Acme. Positive purchase intent nearly tripled from 2025 to 2026 — from 16.75% to 40.75% — but more than half of all respondents across both waves still sit at the neutral midpoint. NPS remains deeply negative at -35. The brand is mid-journey, not at a destination of strength.

**Chart Specification:** Two-element layout.

Element 1 — Stacked horizontal bar showing full purchase intent distribution for 2025 vs. 2026 side by side. Source: `workspace/analysis/banner_tables/banner_year_Q6_purchase_intent.csv`. Values:
- 2025: Negative (1+2) = (1+79)/400 = 20.0%, Neutral (3) = 253/400 = 63.25%, Positive (4+5) = (67+0)/400 = 16.75%
- 2026: Negative (1+2) = (0+24)/400 = 6.0%, Neutral (3) = 213/400 = 53.25%, Positive (4+5) = (156+7)/400 = 40.75%
Color code: red = negative, gray = neutral, green = positive.

Element 2 — NPS call-out box: Overall NPS = -35. Promoters (scores 9-10): 18.625% (149/800). Detractors (scores 0-6): 53.75% (430/800). Source: `workspace/analysis/frequency_tables/freq_Q10_nps.csv`.

**Talking Points:**
- Positive purchase intent nearly tripled in a single year: 16.75% to 40.75% (chi2=76.32, p<0.001). This is real and meaningful momentum.
- But the neutral segment remains large: 63.25% in 2025 to 53.25% in 2026 — declining, but still the majority. Converting this group is the primary growth opportunity.
- NPS overall is -35, which sounds alarming in isolation. The critical context: this is a brand in transition. The 2026 wave is where the promoters are concentrating — score 10 respondents are 81.82% from 2026 (54 of 66). Score 8 respondents are 74.07% from 2026 (80 of 108). Low scores are 2025-heavy: score 2 respondents are 79.41% from 2025 (27 of 34). The NPS distribution is shifting, not static. Source: `workspace/analysis/banner_tables/banner_year_Q10_nps.csv`.
- Among buyers who have already converted, frequency is deepening: weekly-or-more buyers tripled from 3.0% (12/400) to 9.75% (39/400), and combined high-frequency buyers (often + very often) more than doubled from 9.75% (39/400) to 22.5% (90/400), chi2=29.66, p<0.001. Category purchase frequency did not change significantly (p=0.143) — Acme is earning more of the same spend, not riding a category tailwind. Source: `workspace/analysis/banner_tables/banner_year_Q8_acme_frequency.csv`.
- The unconverted majority is broad-based, not segmented: region produced zero significant results, age and gender produced virtually none. This is a brand-wide opportunity, not a demographic targeting problem.

## Slide 7: Insight

**Type:** Insight

**Title:** Trust: The Most Valuable Lever Not Yet Pulled

**Headline Stat:** Trust top-2-box: only 31.0% in 2026 — despite trust being the strongest independent driver beyond quality and freshness

**Narrative:** Trust ranks 4th in raw correlation with purchase intent (r=0.21), but its standardized beta (0.14) is disproportionately high relative to all attributes ranked below it. This means trust has independent predictive power for purchase intent — it drives intent beyond what quality and freshness already explain. Yet despite real year-over-year progress (up from 21.25%), trust top-2-box sits at just 31.0% in 2026, trailing quality (52.5%) and freshness (50.25%) by more than 20 points. The gap between trust's commercial importance and its current score is the single most actionable finding in this study.

**Chart Specification:** Three-element layout. Source files: `workspace/analysis/derived_importance.csv`; `workspace/analysis/banner_tables/banner_year_Q5_10_trust.csv`.

Element 1 — Small bar chart or annotation showing trust's derived importance position relative to quality and freshness:
- Quality: r=0.44, beta=0.34 (Rank 1)
- Freshness: r=0.39, beta=0.30 (Rank 2)
- Trust: r=0.21, beta=0.14 (Rank 4) — highlight the beta-r gap

Element 2 — Year-over-year trust top-2-box comparison (side-by-side bars):
- 2025: scores 4+5 = (74+11)/400 = 85/400 = 21.25%
- 2026: scores 4+5 = (93+31)/400 = 124/400 = 31.0%
- chi2=26.50, p<0.001

Element 3 — Gap callout: "Trust trails quality by 21.5 points and freshness by 19.25 points — despite having comparable independent predictive power for purchase intent."

**Talking Points:**
- Trust is not just another attribute on the list. Its beta coefficient (0.14) tells us it drives purchase intent independently, after quality and freshness have already accounted for what they can explain. Buyers who trust Acme are more likely to purchase — over and above their quality and freshness ratings.
- Despite strong year-over-year improvement (+9.75 pp, chi2=26.50, p<0.001), trust top-2-box at 31.0% in 2026 still means fewer than 1 in 3 buyers strongly agree that Acme is a brand they trust.
- Quality and freshness moved the needle in 2026. They are responsible for the preference flip. Trust is what can lock it in.
- The improvement trajectory is encouraging — trust top-2-box nearly doubled from its absolute base. But closing the remaining gap between trust and the sensory quality attributes is the highest-leverage action available to the brand.
- From the full attribute ranking in `derived_importance.csv`: the three sensory attributes (quality, freshness, sweetness) are likely intercorrelated — trust's independent beta is therefore especially meaningful because it captures something those three attributes do not.

## Slide 8: Takeaway

**Type:** Takeaway

**Title:** What the Data Says Together

**Headline Stat:** Acme earned the #1 preference position. Now it must convert the 58% in the middle.

**Narrative:** The four findings form a single coherent story. Acme's preference flip was earned — it was driven by real product perception improvements on the attributes that statistically drive purchase. The conversion opportunity is large and broad-based: the neutral majority is everywhere, not concentrated in any demographic segment. And the specific lever to pull is trust — the one attribute with disproportionate independent power over purchase intent that still lags far behind quality and freshness.

**Chart Specification:** None — text summary layout only. Display four finding summaries as a connected visual:
1. THE FLIP: Acme overtook Crimson Crown (15.95% → 34.38% preference; +13.5 pp purchase rate)
2. THE REASON: Quality and freshness improved most — exactly the top purchase intent drivers
3. THE OPPORTUNITY: 58.25% remain at the neutral midpoint; NPS = -35 but improving
4. THE LEVER: Trust top-2-box = 31.0% despite being the strongest independent driver beyond sensory quality

Arrow or flow connectors linking each point to the next.

**Talking Points:**
- The preference flip is not fragile — it is rooted in genuine perceptual improvement. That is encouraging for durability.
- But the NPS of -35 signals the brand is not yet generating advocacy at scale. Promoters are concentrating in 2026, which is momentum — but it is early momentum.
- The neutral majority (58.25%) is the prize. These buyers are not opposed to Acme. They are unconvinced. Trust is what moves unconvinced buyers to committed buyers.
- The brand's growth strategy is written in this data: quality signals and freshness proof brought Acme to parity; trust-building is the path to clear leadership.

## Slide 9: Closing

**Type:** Closing

**Title:** Three Actions, One Direction

**Headline Stat:** "Quality and freshness moved the needle. Trust is what converts the remaining 58%."

**Narrative:** The 2026 wave marks a turning point. Acme achieved preference leadership through genuine performance improvement. The next chapter depends on converting the broad neutral majority — and the data is specific about how to do it.

**Chart Specification:** None — text layout with three action items, dark navy background

**Talking Points / Action Items:**

**1. Invest in trust-building communications — it is the highest-ROI lever available.**
Quality (top-2-box 52.5%) and freshness (50.25%) are now strong enough to be credible proof points. The brand's job in 2027 is to translate product reality into brand belief. Trust top-2-box at 31.0% represents a 20+ point gap to close relative to quality and freshness — and trust's independent beta (0.14) means closing it will directly lift purchase intent beyond what further quality/freshness investment alone can achieve. Source: `derived_importance.csv`; `banner_year_Q5_10_trust.csv`.

**2. Protect and extend the preference lead — Crimson Crown is still the purchase-rate leader in absolute terms and is incentivized to respond.**
In 2026, Crimson Crown's purchase rate (35.75%) still exceeds Acme's (42.0%) only narrowly — and Crimson Crown held 48.75% purchase rate in 2025. The competitive switching pattern that brought Acme to the preference lead is fragile if quality/freshness perceptions are not reinforced continuously. Source: `banner_year_Q4_purchased_Acme_Strawberries.csv`; `banner_year_Q4_purchased_Crimson_Crown_Berries.csv`.

**3. Sustain broad-market investment — the neutral majority is not a segment, it is the whole market.**
Zero regional differences and virtually no demographic differences mean there is no efficient niche to target. The 58.25% neutral majority is spread across all geographies, ages, and income groups. Broad-market communications, not targeted messaging, is the structural recommendation. Source: `significance_results.csv`; `freq_Q6_purchase_intent.csv`.

## Slide 10: Appendix Reference

**Type:** Takeaway

**Title:** Methodology & Data Notes

**Headline Stat:** 191 chi-square significance tests | N=800 | Two-wave cross-sectional design

**Narrative:** This slide provides methodology context for informed stakeholders. All findings in this briefing are supported by statistically significant results (p<0.05) unless otherwise noted. Key limitations apply to the NPS and purchase intent cross-tabs.

**Chart Specification:** None — text layout only

**Talking Points:**
- Two-wave cross-sectional design: 400 respondents in 2025, 400 in 2026. This is not a panel — individual respondents were not tracked across waves. Year-over-year comparisons reflect aggregate wave differences, not individual-level change. Causal attribution (e.g., "Crimson Crown buyers switched to Acme") is directionally supported but cannot be proven without a panel design.
- Derived importance analysis: Pearson r and standardized beta coefficients correlate 11 Q5 attributes with Q6 purchase intent across all 800 respondents. The three top sensory attributes are likely intercorrelated; individual beta estimates may be unstable due to multicollinearity. The directional recommendation (invest in sensory quality signals and trust) is robust.
- Q11 brand preference: valid n=754 (46 non-respondents). Per-wave denominators: 2025=370, 2026=384. All preference percentages use these as denominators.
- Expected frequency warnings: Purchase intent x year and NPS x year cross-tabs carry expected frequency warnings due to sparse extreme-score cells. Conclusions are unaffected given the magnitude of the chi-square statistics (76.32 and 130.98 respectively).
- Income x quality: significant (chi2=18.85, p=0.016) but carries an expected frequency warning (min expected cell = 3.14 < 5). Treat with caution.
- Source files: `workspace/analysis/refined_analysis.json`; `workspace/analysis/derived_importance.csv`; `workspace/analysis/significance_results.csv`; all banner and frequency tables in `workspace/analysis/banner_tables/` and `workspace/analysis/frequency_tables/`.
